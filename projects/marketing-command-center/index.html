01 · Problem

Channels agree on almost nothing.

Media platforms, web analytics, CRM, and commerce systems use different naming, grains, currencies, attribution rules, refresh windows, and identifiers.

The command center is designed around a governed semantic layer, not a collection of screenshots. Every headline metric has a definition, source path, owner, freshness expectation, and quality guardrail.

Taxonomy

Same campaign, different names

Naming rules and mapping tables normalize channel, market, objective, and initiative.

Grain

Clicks are not leads

Platform, session, lead, and order data are modeled at explicit grains before joining.

Trust

A total needs lineage

Executives see the number; analysts can trace definition, source, refresh, and quality state.

02 · Architecture

Separate ingestion from meaning.

Raw platform extracts remain intact. Standardization, mapping, deduplication, currency conversion, and metric logic happen in controlled layers before reporting.

04 · Governance

The KPI is a contract.

Each metric includes calculation, grain, inclusions, exclusions, owner, freshness, and guardrails. This prevents a visually polished dashboard from masking incompatible definitions.

01

Primary KPI remains stable

Attribution changes are introduced as clearly named views, not silent replacements.

02

Unknown stays visible

Unmapped campaign values are reported as a quality metric instead of forced into the nearest channel.

03

Currency is controlled

Local platform cost is preserved while EUR reporting uses a dated reference rate.

04

Action has an owner

Every red or amber signal includes the team responsible and the next operational step.
